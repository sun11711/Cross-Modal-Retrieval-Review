Abstract

With the exponential surge in diverse multi-modal data, traditional uni-modal retrieval methods struggle to meet the needs of users seeking access to data across various modalities. To address this, cross-modal retrieval has emerged, enabling interaction across modalities, facilitating semantic matching, and leveraging complementarity and consistency between different modal data. This survey conducts a comprehensive review of cross-modal retrieval's evolution, spanning from traditional visual-semantic embedding methods to vision-language pre-training models, and further to multimodal large language models (MLLMs). Commencing with a comprehensive taxonomy grounded in machine learning paradigms, mechanisms, and models, we delve deeply into the principles and architectures underpinning existing cross-modal retrieval methods. Furthermore, we offer an overview of widely-used benchmarks, metrics, and performances. Lastly, we discuss the prospects and challenges that confront contemporary cross-modal retrieval, while engaging in a discourse on potential directions for further progress in the field.
